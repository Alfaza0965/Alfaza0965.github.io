Let Me Know

In an effort to assist the community during these unprecedented times, Let Me Know is a project under development that is meant to help community members become aware of free services and resources that community leaders are providing as well as being a central point for the many resources in your community.

Compton Advocates

One Compton

Based in Compton, “Compton Advocates” is an organization which encourages action and unity through educating and empowering its residents. Candace, a proud Compton resident, runs its Instagram page, which is a powerful resource that informs about food giveaways, town halls, small businesses, and any event concerning the city of Compton – no piece of knowledge is too trivial. Compton Advocates thrives to “change the negative narratives that have existed for far too long in Compton…to unite black and brown.” To achieve this, it is empirical to educate the Compton community and empower its residents to become advocates themselves, FOR themselves, and for the greater good of Compton.

The Audrey Machine

United we Stand

Audrey Casas’s Instagram page, “The Audrey Machine,” focuses on uplifting urban cities such as Lynwood and Compton. Casas is a Lynwood teacher who has raised over $50,000 to give to those in need; Through the donation of gift cards, groceries, and masks – among other essential items – Casas inspires hope within her community and even beyond her community, reaching over 2,000 followers on Instagram. Casas has posted her Zelle, CashApp, and Venmo information on Instagram to accept donations to extend the distribution of hope. With real stories and real images of real people, “The Audrey Machine” highlights the power of giving, from a little girl who lit up at the sight of a Lucky Charms cereal box, to a laptop donation from the piggy bank savings of two kids from Washington State, to a family who used their $100 grocery gift card to start up their own menudo business – no donation is ever too small.

Invisible Men

Strength in Will

Luckie Fuller, founder of the Instagram page “Invisible Transmen,” offers a platform for “trans men and trans masculine individuals to have the ability to tell their own narratives instead being handed who they were according to the world.” Fuller advocates for transmen through holding panel discussions, virtual balls (called “Legacy Balls”), and workshops. Fuller also designed an “All Black Lives Matter” installation on Hollywood Blvd. Fuller recognizes the importance of having a safe space for voices to be heard – voices that have been invisible for too long.
